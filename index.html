CraftSpace

Pick a craft, follow the tutorial, walk into a studio that's already set up for you. CraftSpace connects your phone to a smart board so beginners spend less time figuring out and more time making.

Why We Built This

Why CraftSpace Exists

Most beginners give up before they start — too many options, no clear guidance, and shared studio spaces that feel intimidating and inaccessible.

CraftSpace bridges your phone and the studio smart board so you always know what to make, what to grab, and who to ask.

Our Process

How we designed it

From 21 user interviews and a makerspace visit to a fully interactive prototype.

User Research

We surveyed 21 young adults (ages 18–25) and conducted contextual inquiry at The MILL makerspace. Most participants are beginners learning at home through online tutorials, with frustrations around isolation, lack of structure, and intimidating shared spaces.

Limited Support for Beginners 90% of learners practice at home using online tutorials. Beginners lack accessible guidance and feel hesitant to ask for help.
Fear of Judgment A welcoming, non-judgmental environment is critical. Supportive peers and mentors significantly reduce intimidation and fear of failure.
Ineffective Shared Spaces Crowding, long wait times, and inconsistent tools create barriers. Most beginners prefer learning at home despite limited resources.

Paper Prototype

Built low-fidelity prototypes to test the craft selection and class enrollment flows with users. Key insight: progress tracking was important.

Digital Mockup (V1)

Created a fully interactive Figma prototype with complete user flows — craft browsing, equipment reservation, class enrollment, progress tracking, and mentor connections.
